Beginning to Crochet

Are you looking to learn to crochet? Take a look at these resources to learn more!

The following list of resources helped me to learn to crochet:

  • Read patterns - Research free crochet patterns for amigurumi, sweaters, or other projects to see what stitches are used. Ravelry has many free patterns, but you need to make a free account to search for them.
  • Research general how-tos - Search for videos on how to crochet in general to find lots of useful tips. These videos go over how to hold your supplies and how to do a simple stitch.
  • Research common beginner's traps - Search for information on common beginner mistakes. Learning from others' mistakes is a great way to quickly advance your own skills!
  • Research any stitch you read about for the first time - When you read patterns on Ravelry or another place, you may come across abbreviations for stitches you don't know--sc for single crochet, dc for double crochet, etc. You should search for what these terms mean and learn how to do them!
  • Research stitch abbreviations - Speaking of learning new stitches, you could research common stitches beforehand to at least learn their names!
  • Try to practice a lot! - Once you learn enough stitches to create projects (you may only need the single crochet and the slip knot!), it is easier to practice more and more. Working towards the creation of a project gives you a nice goal to work towards and counts as practice!
